Who We Are

"|| जागृत मातृ-पितृ गुरुजन शक्ती तव नाम राष्ट्रोन्नती ||"

Established in 1953 Bharatiya Stree Jeevan Vikas Parishad is a non-profit social organization committed to the holistic development of women and society. The organization works in the fields of education, healthcare support, vocational training, women’s welfare, and community development. Over the years, the Parishad has developed various initiatives aimed at strengthening communities and creating sustainable opportunities for women and children.

The objectives of Bhartiya Stree Jeevan Vikas Parishad are to educate Bhartiya women and facilitated them to be self-supporting. To fulfil these objectives parishad established “Gruhini Vidyalay” at Mahim. Since 1965, for almost 20-25 yrs, the parishad has been running Govt. recognised certificate & diploma course in home science at Mahim. Along with this the parishad is also running short courses such as: Tailoring & Embroidery, Bakery and Cooking, Photography, Batick, Yogasanas, Typing, Interior Decoration and Draftsman etc. The Gruhini Vidyalay was given an opportunity by the Government of Maharashtra to participate in Applied Nutrition program, the UNISEF scheme, for 4 years since 1965. The parishad used to supply food to students from 4 schools run by social welfare board and many other schools. Currently the parishad is running a working women hostel and “kamalini karnabadhir Vidyalaya” at Thane.

Government of maharashtra has given opportunity to the Parishad to impart value education in rural ashramshalas. Hence project for tribal students is being held for personality development of the tribal students. Our volunteers in the organization involved in this project go to verious Ashram schools in all over Maharashtra and teach them Yoga, games, physical activities, shloka, stories etc.
• Apart from conducting Yoga and fitness centre, this organization also conduct Sanskar Varga for children.
• This organisation has also been working towards countering malnutrition in the tribal areas by arranging medical camps in the tribal areas.

About the Program

"Empowering women through education, sanskar, and self-reliance"

Social work


With the guidance of the Rashtra Sevika Samiti and the cooperation of the Bharatiya Stree Jeevan Vikas Parishad, Thane. Since 2007, Rashtra Sevika Samiti, Bharatiya Stree Jeevan Vikas Parishad, Thane has been implementing a special project for the education of girls in rural and tribal areas along with the development of urban women.

The organization started patient assistant course for tribal girls in Thane district. Girls who pass 12th with above fifty percent marks are trained in ANM Auxiliary and Midwifery Nursing at SNDT University for 2 years. Accommodation, food, travel, education are provided free of charge by the organization for these girls. Experienced doctors and nurses are helpful in training the girls. Hands-on practice, experience is provided from nearby hospitals. Till date 160 girls have taken education from this institution. All those girls carry good manners and outlook on life and are living in the society by being self-reliant. They show the direction of health and cleanliness to the tribal and rural communities, which are dominated by superstitions.

For these girls, accommodation, food, travel, and education facilities are provided free of cost by the institution.

Experienced doctors and nurses voluntarily participate in the girls’ training. Opportunities for practical training and experience are provided through hospitals. After completing the course, most of the girls work in their own villages while earning a livelihood.

About This Program

"Teach a child to speak by first helping them hear."

Kamlini Karnabadhir Vidyalaya, Thane (East), has been serving hearing-impaired children since 1990.
Currently, about 60 students from pre-primary to seventh standard are receiving education in the school.

Since 2017, the school has been running an Early Intervention Department. Through this department, children are admitted at a very young age and are guided for cochlear implant surgery when appropriate. Speech and language development training is provided. By using these interventions, the school successfully integrated 4 students in 2019 and 1 student in 2022 into mainstream schools. These students have continued their education successfully and have passed school examinations.

Apart from academic education, students are also given opportunities to learn vocational and life skills. Training is provided in:
• Photography
• Fabric painting
• Warli painting
• Jewellery making from paper quilling
• Hand embroidery
• Tailoring and other practical skills

Various social awareness and community participation activities are also conducted for students. Students appear for scholarship examinations and achieve commendable results. They participate in many competitions and receive prizes and recognition. After completing secondary education, students are encouraged to pursue higher education and become self-reliant. Many former students are now professionally employed and leading happy family and social lives.

More details:
• Along with academic education, the students are also provided with pre-vocational training. These students possess IQ, SQ, and EQ levels comparable to those of other mainstream students, and they are excelling by developing their own unique abilities.
• Additionally, for children in the 0-3 years age group, parents are guided regarding cochlear implant surgery. Properly trained students are given opportunities for inclusive education in mainstream schools.
• Speech and hearing training for children aged 0–5 years, guidance for cochlear implants, AVT (Auditory Verbal Therapy) services, and parent counseling.
• Specialized speech and hearing rooms with expert guidance for speech and hearing development.
• Computer classes, career guidance, and employment-related support.
• Mid-day meal facilities, educational books, and learning materials provided to students.
• Students are given opportunities to participate in Maharashtra State-level art examinations.
• Continuous efforts are made for the all-round development of students.
